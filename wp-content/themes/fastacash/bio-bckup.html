Vince Tallent - Chairman & CEO

Vince has eighteen years of extensive experience within the telecommunications, media & technology industries. Vince has worked with Public and Private Company Boards and their shareholders, who have been either Venture Capitalists, Private Equity or Public Market investors / Institutions. The focus has been on international business within the mobile and payments industries, delivering service offerings such as payment solutions, mobile airtime transfer, mobile data roaming and mobile applications within developed and emerging markets such as Africa, Middle East, India, Indonesia and China. Vince has held a series of Board Level and senior management positions. The companies have been at various stages of their development from early stage Venture Capital backed companies such as OmniSky, Mobileway and Mobile365 to more late stage companies backed by Private Equity such as Mach and Mobile Systems International, to large Publicly quoted companies such as Ingenico, MediaOne, US West and British Telecommunications. Vince was recently the EVP & MD of Ingenico Asia Pacific, he was also Chief Corporate Development & Strategy Officer at Ingenico and ran Ingenico Ventures. Vince was also a main board member of Ingenico, where he was Chairman of the Strategy Committee, a member of the Audit Committee, and a member of the Ethics and Governance Committee. He also held board positions at Roamdata and Transfer-To, and held executive board member positions at Mach and Mobile365 where he was the Group CFO.

Shankar is a seasoned Payments professional with a successful track record in innovation, communications, strategy development, technology, operations and product development. He has strong leadership, problem solving, change management and project management skills and a key strength is his ability to develop strategic alliances. Peter Harkin - Chief Financial Officer

Samuel Leinhardt

Dr. Samuel Leinhardt is a successful entrepreneur, scientist, and academic. Throughout the course of his career, Leinhardt has founded several companies including Leinhardt-McCormick Associates, a supplier of software tools for exploratory data analysis and data mining, and Formtek, Inc., a developer of Computer Assisted Design and Engineering Document Management systems. In 1989, after Lockheed Martin acquired Formtek, he served as a Vice President at Lockheed Martin. Leinhardt co-founded and served as President and CEO of Storm, LLC, a developer of file management products for the web. He then joined Eizel Technologies, Inc., as CEO in 2001. A developer of mobile email systems, Eizel was acquired by Nokia in 2003. Leinhardt served as a Vice President at Nokia until 2004. In 2005 he co-founded Penthera Technologies, Inc., and served as its President and CEO. In 2008, Leinhardt became the Chairman of Socure, Inc., a provider of fraud detection products and services based on social graph analytics. Leinhardt’s BA, MA and PhD degrees are from the University of Chicago. He was a Lecturer at Harvard University in mathematical social science and Professor at Carnegie Mellon University’s Tepper School of Business and Heinz School of Public Policy from 1969-1983. He is currently Adjunct Professor of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon’s Human Computer Interaction Institute in the School of Computer Science. Leinhardt is the author and editor of numerous academic books, articles and journals in sociology, economics, econometrics, mathematical statistics and social networks.

Enoch Chng

Enoch is a former Executive Director of the Monetary Authority of Singapore and headed the 44-person Specialist Risk Supervision Department, which provides the financial, and technology risk expertise necessary for supervisory and regulatory functions, and assessment of institution-specific and system-wide risks. The department also oversees payment and settlement infrastructures with the objective of fostering their integrity, stability and efficiency. Enoch has represented the MAS in the Bank for International Settlements (BIS) Committee on Payment and Settlement Systems (CPSS), and Executives’ Meeting of East Asia–Pacific Central Banks and Monetary Authorities (EMEAP) Working Group on Payment and Settlement Systems. He is currently an Associate Professor of Information Systems (Education) at the Singapore Management University. He is also the Director of the School of Information Systems Programs in Financial Services (Technology, Operations, Processes, and Service Innovations).
